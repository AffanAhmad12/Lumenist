1. PURPOSE
This policy defines the standards of conduct expected from Lumelight employees and the
process followed when those standards are not met. It aims to ensure fair, consistent, and
transparent handling of misconduct while protecting the rights of all employees involved.

2. SCOPE
Applies to all confirmed and probationary employees of Lumelight. Interns and contractors are
covered under separate clauses referenced in their engagement letters, but the general
principles of fairness described here apply to them as well.

3. CATEGORIES OF MISCONDUCT

3.1 Minor Misconduct (examples)
- Habitual late coming or early leaving without approval
- Unexcused absenteeism (1-2 instances)
- Failure to follow standard operating procedures
- Misuse of company email for non-work purposes
- Minor breach of dress code

3.2 Major Misconduct (examples)
- Repeated minor misconduct without improvement after warning
- Insubordination or refusal to carry out reasonable work instructions
- Breach of client confidentiality or data protection norms
- Falsification of timesheets, expense claims, or company records
- Misuse of company assets or systems
- Behavior that violates the Code of Conduct

3.3 Gross Misconduct (examples)
- Theft, fraud, or financial dishonesty
- Physical violence or threats of violence at the workplace
- Sexual harassment (handled under the POSH Policy)
- Working under the influence of alcohol or banned substances
- Serious breach of data security leading to client or business harm
- Conviction for a criminal offense relevant to employment

4. DISCIPLINARY PROCESS

Step 1: Verbal Counseling
For first-time minor issues, the reporting manager provides verbal feedback and documents the
conversation in the employee's file.

Step 2: Written Warning
If the issue recurs or is more serious, a written warning is issued by the manager with HR
review, specifying the concern and expected corrective action within a defined timeline
(typically 30 days).

Step 3: Show Cause Notice
For major misconduct, HR issues a formal show cause notice asking the employee to explain
their conduct in writing within 3-5 working days.

Step 4: Disciplinary Inquiry
A committee (manager + HR representative, and a senior leader for gross misconduct cases)
reviews the explanation, may interview witnesses, and records findings.

Step 5: Decision and Action
Based on the inquiry findings, HR will recommend one of the following:
- No action / case closed
- Final written warning
- Suspension without pay (up to 15 days)
- Demotion or transfer
- Termination of employment

5. SUSPENSION PENDING INQUIRY
In cases of alleged gross misconduct, an employee may be placed under suspension (with or
without pay, per applicable law and contract terms) while the inquiry is ongoing, to maintain
workplace safety and the integrity of the investigation.

6. EMPLOYEE RIGHTS DURING THE PROCESS
- Right to be informed of the specific allegation in writing
- Right to respond and present their version of events
- Right to be accompanied by a colleague during disciplinary meetings (on request)
- Right to appeal the decision within 7 working days of receiving it

7. APPEAL PROCESS
An employee may appeal a disciplinary decision by writing to the Head of HR within 7 working
days. The appeal will be reviewed by a leader not involved in the original decision, and a final
response will be provided within 15 working days.

8. RECORD KEEPING
All disciplinary actions are recorded in the employee's personnel file and retained as per
Lumelight's data retention schedule. Verbal counseling records are typically retained for 12
months; written warnings and above are retained for the duration of employment plus the
statutory period thereafter.

9. CONFIDENTIALITY
Disciplinary proceedings are handled on a need-to-know basis. Details are not shared with
colleagues outside the process unless required for the inquiry or by law.
