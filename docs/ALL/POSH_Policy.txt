LUMELIGHT — PREVENTION OF SEXUAL HARASSMENT (POSH) POLICY
Document Owner: Human Resources
Effective Date: 1 April 2026
Last Reviewed: 1 April 2026
Next Review Due: 31 March 2027
Document Version: 1.0
Applicable To: All employees, contractors, interns, vendors, and visitors at Lumelight premises

1. PURPOSE
Lumelight is committed to providing a safe, respectful, and inclusive workplace for all employees,
regardless of gender, gender identity, or sexual orientation. This policy outlines Lumelight's zero
tolerance approach to sexual harassment at the workplace and establishes the mechanism for
reporting, investigating, and resolving complaints, in line with the Sexual Harassment of Women
at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013.

2. SCOPE
This policy applies to all employees of Lumelight (permanent, probationary, contractual, and
temporary), interns, consultants, and third-party vendors working at or visiting any Lumelight
office, client site, or work-from-home setup, including conduct during official travel and
work-related social events.

3. DEFINITION OF SEXUAL HARASSMENT
Sexual harassment includes any one or more of the following unwelcome acts or behavior,
whether directly or by implication:
- Physical contact and advances of a sexual nature
- A demand or request for sexual favors
- Sexually colored remarks or jokes
- Showing pornography or sexually explicit content
- Any other unwelcome physical, verbal, or non-verbal conduct of a sexual nature
- Creating a hostile work environment through persistent unwelcome behavior
- Online or digital harassment, including inappropriate messages, emails, or social media conduct
  related to a colleague

4. INTERNAL COMMITTEE (IC)
Lumelight has constituted an Internal Committee to receive and inquire into complaints of sexual
harassment. The Internal Committee comprises:
- A Presiding Officer (senior woman employee)
- Two employee members committed to the cause of women
- One external member from an NGO or association familiar with issues of sexual harassment

Contact details of the current Internal Committee members are published on the HR intranet
page and circulated via email at the start of every financial year.

5. HOW TO RAISE A COMPLAINT
- A written complaint may be submitted to any Internal Committee member within 3 months of
  the incident (extendable at the Committee's discretion).
- Complaints may be submitted via email, the HR portal complaint form, or in a sealed envelope
  addressed to the Presiding Officer.
- The complainant may be assisted in writing the complaint if they are unable to do so themselves.
- All complaints will be acknowledged within 2 working days of receipt.

6. INQUIRY PROCESS
- The Internal Committee will complete the inquiry within 90 days of receiving the complaint.
- Both parties will be given an opportunity to be heard and to present evidence/witnesses.
- During the inquiry, the Committee may recommend interim measures such as transfer of either
  party or granting leave to the complainant, up to 3 months.
- The identity of the complainant, respondent, and witnesses will be kept strictly confidential
  throughout the process.

7. ACTION ON FINDINGS
- If the allegation is substantiated, the Committee will recommend disciplinary action as per the
  Lumelight Disciplinary Policy, which may include written warning, suspension, demotion, or
  termination, depending on severity.
- If the complaint is found to be false or malicious, action may be initiated against the
  complainant as per applicable policy, after due inquiry.
- A copy of the inquiry report and recommendations will be shared with both parties and the
  Employer.

8. NON-RETALIATION
Lumelight strictly prohibits retaliation against any employee who raises a complaint in good faith
or participates in an inquiry as a witness. Any act of retaliation will itself be treated as
misconduct and will attract disciplinary action.

9. CONFIDENTIALITY
All details relating to a POSH complaint, including identities of parties and the proceedings of
the inquiry, must be kept strictly confidential by all individuals involved. Breach of confidentiality
is a disciplinary offense.

10. TRAINING AND AWARENESS
- All new employees must complete a mandatory POSH awareness module during onboarding.
- Refresher training is conducted annually for all employees.
- Internal Committee members undergo specialized training on handling inquiries sensitively.

11. ANNUAL REPORT
Lumelight HR will submit an annual report on the number of POSH complaints received,
disposed of, and pending, to the District Officer as required under law, and will publish a
summary internally (without identifying details) for transparency.

12. CONTACT
For questions about this policy or to report a concern, contact:

- HR Helpdesk: hr.helpdesk@lumelight.com (demo address)
