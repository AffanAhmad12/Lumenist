LUMELIGHT — DRESS CODE POLICY
Document Owner: Human Resources
Effective Date: 1 April 2026
Last Reviewed: 1 April 2026
Next Review Due: 31 March 2027
Document Version: 1.0
Applicable To: All employees across BPO, LPO, HR, Finance, IT, and support functions

1. PURPOSE
This policy outlines the dress and grooming standards expected of Lumelight employees to
maintain a professional, safe, and respectful work environment, while presenting a consistent
brand image to clients and visitors.

2. GENERAL PRINCIPLE
Employees are expected to dress in a manner that is clean, neat, and appropriate for a
professional office or client-facing environment. Clothing should not be offensive, overly casual,
or a safety hazard.

3. STANDARD OFFICE ATTIRE (MONDAY-THURSDAY)

3.1 For all employees
- Business casual attire is the standard: collared shirts, blouses, trousers, skirts, or formal
  dresses
- Closed or semi-formal footwear is preferred; clean sandals are acceptable in non-client-facing
  roles
- Clothing should be well-fitted, clean, and free of visible tears or excessive wear

3.2 For client-facing and BPO voice process employees
- Formal business attire is mandatory: shirts/blouses with trousers or formal skirts, and
  closed-toe formal shoes
- Lumelight-issued ID badges must be worn visibly at all times during working hours

4. CASUAL FRIDAYS
Employees in non-client-facing roles may wear smart casual attire on Fridays, including
collared t-shirts, jeans (without rips or excessive fading), and clean sneakers. Client-facing
teams should check with their manager, as casual Friday norms may not apply during client
visits or audits.

5. ITEMS NOT PERMITTED
- Shorts, flip-flops, or beachwear
- Clothing with offensive graphics, language, or political slogans
- Visible undergarments
- Strong perfumes/colognes in shared or close-contact workspaces (in consideration of
  colleagues with sensitivities)
- Torn, faded, or overly casual gym wear

6. GROOMING STANDARDS
- Hair should be clean and neatly maintained
- Visible tattoos and piercings are generally acceptable unless they are offensive in nature or
  restricted by a specific client contract
- Personal hygiene should be maintained given close-proximity workstations

7. RELIGIOUS AND CULTURAL ATTIRE
Lumelight respects religious and cultural diversity. Traditional or religious attire (such as
turbans, hijabs, bindis, or sacred threads) is welcomed and does not conflict with this policy.

8. WORK-FROM-HOME / VIRTUAL MEETINGS
Employees attending video calls with clients or senior leadership should dress in business
casual attire at minimum, even when working remotely. Internal team calls allow for more
relaxed attire, provided it remains appropriate for a professional setting.

9. SAFETY-RELATED EXCEPTIONS
Employees working in technical, facilities, or warehouse areas (if applicable) must follow
additional safety-specific dress requirements (e.g., closed shoes, no loose jewelry) as
communicated by their department head.

10. NON-COMPLIANCE
Repeated non-compliance with the dress code, after verbal feedback from a manager, may
result in escalation under the Disciplinary Policy.

11. EXCEPTIONS
Any exception required due to medical, religious, or cultural reasons should be discussed with
the reporting manager and HR, and will be accommodated wherever reasonably possible.

12. CONTACT
For questions about this policy, contact HR Helpdesk: hr.helpdesk@lumelight.com (demo address)
