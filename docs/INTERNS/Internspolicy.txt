# Internship Policy

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Internship Policy is to establish clear guidelines for interns regarding their responsibilities, conduct, attendance, confidentiality, and overall internship experience at **[Company Name]**. This policy ensures a professional, productive, and mutually beneficial learning environment.

---

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to all interns engaged with **[Company Name]**, regardless of whether the internship is paid or unpaid, remote, hybrid, or on-site.

---

# 3. Internship Duration

* Internship duration will be specified in the Internship Offer Letter.
* Any extension of the internship is at the sole discretion of the company.
* Completion of the internship does not guarantee permanent employment.

---

# 4. Working Hours

* Interns are expected to work according to the schedule assigned by their reporting manager.
* Standard working hours are **[e.g., Monday to Friday, 9:30 AM – 6:30 PM]** unless otherwise communicated.
* Interns are expected to be punctual and maintain regular attendance.

---

# 5. Attendance

* Attendance is mandatory throughout the internship period.
* Interns must mark attendance using the company's designated system.
* Frequent absenteeism or late arrivals may result in disciplinary action or termination of the internship.

---

# 6. Leave Policy

* Leave requests must be submitted to the reporting manager in advance whenever possible.
* Emergency leave must be communicated as soon as practicable.
* Excessive or unauthorized leave may affect internship evaluation, certification, or stipend eligibility.

---

# 7. Code of Conduct

Interns are expected to:

* Maintain professionalism at all times.
* Treat colleagues, clients, and visitors with respect.
* Follow all company rules and policies.
* Maintain a positive and cooperative attitude.
* Perform assigned work honestly and responsibly.
* Avoid any behavior that could harm the company's reputation.

---

# 8. Confidentiality

During the internship, interns may have access to confidential information, including but not limited to:

* Client information
* Business strategies
* Financial information
* Technical documentation
* Source code
* Internal communications

Interns must not disclose, copy, distribute, or misuse confidential information during or after the internship without written authorization.

---

# 9. Information Security

Interns must:

* Keep passwords secure.
* Use only authorized software and systems.
* Avoid downloading unauthorized applications.
* Report any suspected security incidents immediately.
* Follow all cybersecurity guidelines issued by the company.

---

# 10. Company Assets

Any company assets provided to the intern, such as laptops, ID cards, software licenses, access cards, or other equipment:

* Must be used only for official purposes.
* Must be handled with reasonable care.
* Must be returned in good condition upon completion or termination of the internship.

---

# 11. Performance Evaluation

Intern performance may be evaluated based on:

* Attendance and punctuality
* Quality of work
* Learning ability
* Communication skills
* Teamwork
* Professional conduct
* Timely completion of assigned tasks

Performance reviews may be conducted periodically during the internship.

---

# 12. Stipend

If applicable:
* IF applicable they  will get 10000 stipend per month
* The stipend amount and payment schedule will be communicated in the Internship Offer Letter.
* The company reserves the right to withhold or adjust stipend payments in cases of policy violations, excessive absenteeism, or early termination, subject to applicable laws and the internship agreement.

---

# 13. Intellectual Property

Any work, software, documentation, research, designs, inventions, reports, or other materials created during the internship shall remain the exclusive property of **[Company Name]**, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

---

# 14. Anti-Harassment and Equal Opportunity

The company is committed to providing a safe, respectful, and inclusive workplace.

Discrimination, harassment, bullying, intimidation, or retaliation based on race, religion, gender, age, disability, or any other protected characteristic will not be tolerated.

Interns should report any such incidents to the HR department immediately.

---

# 15. Social Media Policy

Interns shall not:

* Share confidential company information online.
* Represent personal opinions as official company statements.
* Publish company data, client information, or internal discussions without authorization.

---

# 16. Termination of Internship

The company reserves the right to terminate the internship at any time for reasons including, but not limited to:

* Violation of company policies.
* Misconduct or unethical behavior.
* Breach of confidentiality.
* Poor performance.
* Unauthorized absence.
* Misuse of company resources.

The intern may also resign by providing reasonable prior notice, unless otherwise stated in the internship agreement.

---

# 17. Internship Completion

Upon successful completion of the internship, interns may receive:

* Internship Completion Certificate
* Experience Letter (if applicable)
* Performance Evaluation (if applicable)

Issuance of these documents is subject to satisfactory completion of assigned responsibilities and return of all company property.

---
