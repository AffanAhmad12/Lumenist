# Finance Policy

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Finance Policy is to establish standardized financial procedures, promote accountability, ensure accurate financial recordkeeping, and safeguard the financial assets of **[Company Name]**.

---

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, interns, consultants, contractors, and departments involved in financial activities or the use of company funds.

---

# 3. Financial Responsibility

The Finance Department is responsible for:

* Maintaining accurate financial records.
* Processing payments and receipts.
* Managing company budgets.
* Preparing financial reports.
* Ensuring compliance with applicable tax and financial regulations.
* Safeguarding company assets.

All employees are responsible for using company funds responsibly and complying with this policy.

---

# 4. Budget Management

* Each department shall operate within its approved budget.
* Expenditures exceeding the approved budget require prior written approval from management.
* Budget reviews shall be conducted periodically.

---

# 5. Revenue Management

All company income must:

* Be accurately recorded.
* Be deposited into authorized company bank accounts.
* Be supported by invoices, receipts, or other appropriate documentation.

No employee may receive company payments into personal bank accounts.

---

# 6. Expense Policy

Employees may claim reimbursement only for approved business-related expenses.

Examples include:

* Business travel
* Client meetings
* Office supplies
* Software subscriptions
* Training programs
* Company-approved purchases

Personal expenses will not be reimbursed.

All expense claims must include valid supporting documents such as invoices or receipts.

---

# 7. Procurement and Purchasing

* Purchases must be approved before being made.
* Procurement should consider quality, price, and vendor reliability.
* Multiple quotations may be required for high-value purchases.
* Unauthorized purchases may not be reimbursed.

---

# 8. Petty Cash

Petty cash may be used only for minor operational expenses.

* Every petty cash transaction must be supported by a receipt.
* The petty cash register shall be reconciled regularly.
* Cash shortages or discrepancies must be reported immediately.

---

# 9. Payment Authorization

Payments shall be processed only after obtaining the required approvals.

Typical approval levels:

* Department Head
* Finance Department
* Managing Director / CEO (for large-value payments)

No employee may authorize payments to themselves.

---

# 10. Payroll and Stipends

Employee salaries and intern stipends shall be processed according to the company's payroll schedule.

Payroll shall include:

* Salary or stipend
* Approved incentives
* Statutory deductions (where applicable)
* Reimbursements

Unauthorized changes to payroll are prohibited.

---

# 11. Reimbursement Process

Employees seeking reimbursement must submit:

* Expense claim form
* Original receipts or invoices
* Purpose of the expense
* Required approvals

Reimbursements will be processed according to the company's payment cycle.

---

# 12. Record Keeping

Financial records including:

* Invoices
* Bills
* Receipts
* Bank statements
* Tax documents
* Payroll records
* Expense reports

shall be maintained securely for the period required by applicable laws.

---

# 13. Asset Management

All company assets shall be properly recorded and monitored.

Assets include:

* Computers
* Laptops
* Servers
* Office equipment
* Furniture
* Software licenses
* Vehicles (if applicable)

Periodic asset verification may be conducted.

---

# 14. Fraud Prevention

Employees shall not:

* Submit false expense claims.
* Create fake invoices.
* Misuse company funds.
* Falsify financial records.
* Accept unauthorized commissions or kickbacks.
* Make unauthorized financial commitments on behalf of the company.

Any suspected fraud must be reported immediately to management.

---

# 15. Financial Confidentiality

Financial information is confidential.

Employees must not disclose:

* Company revenue
* Profit margins
* Financial statements
* Vendor pricing
* Payroll information
* Banking details
* Client financial information

without proper authorization.

---

# 16. Audit

The company may conduct:

* Internal audits
* External audits
* Financial inspections
* Compliance reviews

Employees are expected to cooperate fully during audit activities.

---

# 17. Tax Compliance

The company shall comply with all applicable tax laws and statutory requirements.

Employees must submit any required financial or tax-related documents promptly when requested.

---

# 18. Conflict of Interest

Employees shall disclose any financial interest that could influence company purchasing or financial decisions.

Transactions involving related parties must receive prior approval from management.

---

# 19. Policy Violations

Violation of this Finance Policy may result in disciplinary action, including:

* Written warning
* Recovery of financial losses
* Suspension
* Termination of employment or internship
* Legal action where applicable
